Intro

At first glance, the internet must seem like magic! One second you are staring at a blank screen, and within a moment or two you could be watching cat videos, or talking to people halfway across the world (and without long-distance fees!). But how do computers pull off this seemingly amazing feat?

I'm about to use some big words here, but hopefully by the time you've read this whole page, you will have a good understanding of what this means. For this example, let's pretend you just booted up your computer and typed "google.com" into your browser. First, your computer checks with a DNS server because your computer doesn't naturally understand human language. This DNS server takes "google.com" and turns it into an address, called an IP address. This address acts like home addresses work for us, it tells the computer where it needs to go next. So this computer sends a request to this address, and the servers at this address present your computer with something called an HTML file, and it's accompanying files. The browser then has to take this file, and turns it into something that looks nice to humans like us. Some websites have extra capability, like a live chat, or telling you the weather. This is usually done using a language called JavaScript. This language can be used to make small programs that your computer executes to make the website more interactive.
